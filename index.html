Key Features:

  1. Salary Analysis: Explore average salaries across different experience levels, from entry-level positions to senior roles.

  3. Specializations: Discover the salary variations among various data science specializations, including machine learning, data engineering, and business analytics.
  4. Company Size Comparison: Compare salaries between large corporations and small to medium-sized enterprises in the data science domain.
  5. Top-Paying Jobs: Uncover the most lucrative positions in data science and gain insights into the skillsets and qualifications that contribute to their high compensation.
  6. Average Salary by Job: Explore the average salaries across different job roles within the field of data science, enabling professionals and employers to gauge competitive compensation levels for specific positions.

  8. Average Job by Remote Ratio: Evaluate the average ratio of remote work opportunities in different data science positions. This information is crucial for professionals seeking remote work options and organizations looking to adapt to evolving work preferences.
  9. Average Salary by Company Size: Assess the average salaries in data science based on the size of the employing organizations. This feature helps professionals and employers understand the impact of company size on compensation structures and provides insights for competitive positioning.

By leveraging the power of Power BI, this project offers an engaging and dynamic exploration of data science salaries, providing valuable information for aspiring data scientists, professionals in the field, and hiring managers alike.

Solar Photovoltaic Project Analysis: Technical and Economic Factors for Feasibility Assessment

My Solar Photovoltaic Project Analysis project in Excel provides a comprehensive framework for evaluating the technical and economic aspects of implementing a solar photovoltaic (PV) project. Designed to assist in the decision-making process, this project offers calculations for determining the required number of solar modules, power output, and inverters based on project requirements. Additionally, it includes a financial analysis sheet that assesses the project's feasibility and provides key financial metrics.

Key Features:

  1. Technical Calculations: Calculate the required number of solar modules and power output based on the project's specifications and energy requirements. These calculations help ensure accurate sizing and configuration of the PV system for optimal performance.
  2. Financial Analysis: Evaluate the economic viability of the solar PV project using various financial parameters and assumptions. The financial analysis sheet incorporates factors such as rated DC power, specific yield in the first year, electric rate, annual fee increase, PV module degradation, annual O&M cost, discount rate for NPV, annual inflation, specific cost of capital, capital cost, debt percentage, debt amount, own funds, annual loan interest rate, loan term, capital recovery factor, annual fee, capital distribution, annual insurance, project useful life, and change of inverters. The financial analysis provides essential metrics, including Net Present Value (NPV), Internal Rate of Return (IRR), Return on Investment (ROI), and Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE).

This Excel-based project offers a user-friendly interface, allowing users to input their project-specific data and instantly obtain financial insights. By analyzing the economic factors and generating key financial metrics, businesses and project stakeholders can determine the project's financial feasibility and assess its long-term profitability.

The Solar Photovoltaic Project Analysis project helps users make informed decisions by providing a comprehensive understanding of the technical requirements and financial implications associated with solar PV projects. It offers a reliable tool for evaluating project viability, optimizing resource allocation, and assessing the financial return on investment.

This Solar Photovoltaic Project Analysis project helps to streamline your decision-making process, mitigate risks, and ensure the successful implementation of sustainable and financially viable solar PV projects.
